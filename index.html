The Foodi Food history

More than 25+ years of experience

That smell of smoked pastrami and kosher dill pickles that hits you when you walk into the legendary 'Foodi Food' is the same smell that’s permeated the bustling, noisy and delightfully frenetic restaurant for decades. What opened in 1888 as a modest deli known for its house-made sausages is now one of the marquee attractions in Manhattan’s Lower East Side and one of the few remaining bastions that defined the vibrant Yiddish-speaking Jewish community that once flourished there.
